    How To Evaluate And Improve Your Writing

    It’s not enough to brain-dump ideas onto a Google Doc.

    You need to review your work and improve it until you are confident it will achieve the desired results.

    Writing is an iterative process:

    Observe → Orient → Decide → Act → Observe → Orient → Decide → Act →, etc.


    Step 1: Read your copy out loud. Using your voice allows you to detach enough from creating your copy to identify issues.
    You’ll notice clunky or weird parts when read out loud.

    Step 2: Imagine how the Avatar will perceive the writing “What effect will this create in the reader’s mind?”
    “What can I do to trigger the effect I want in the reader’s mind here?”

    Step 3: Grammar and spelling check. Grammar and spelling errors will make you look like an amateur in your reader's eyes.
    “If this is how badly he writes, I wonder how bad the product he’s selling is?”
    (Use grammarly.com, hemingwayapp.com, and other tools to ensure perfect grammar and spelling.)
